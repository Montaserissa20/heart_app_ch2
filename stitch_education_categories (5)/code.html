Initial Recovery: Day 1 & 2

After your heart surgery, the first 24 to 48 hours are focused on stabilization. You will likely wake up in the Intensive Care Unit (ICU) where medical staff will monitor your vitals closely.

air Breathing Exercises

It is crucial to use your incentive spirometer every hour while awake. This helps prevent lung complications such as pneumonia. Take a deep breath in through the device, hold for 3 seconds, and exhale slowly.

directions_walk Early Mobility

The nursing team will assist you in sitting on the edge of the bed or moving to a chair. Movement is essential for circulation and digestive health. Always use your heart pillow to "splint" your chest when moving or coughing.

"Recovery is a marathon, not a sprint. Take small steps each day and communicate any discomfort with your nurse immediately."

Your pain will be managed through a combination of medications. Inform your care team if your pain level exceeds a 4 out of 10.
